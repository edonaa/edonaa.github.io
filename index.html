About me

PhD candidate in Computer Science, with a research focus on computer vision, deep learning, semantic segmentation, and Extended Reality (XR) to support structural damage assessment in disaster and emergency management.

PhD candidate in the field of Computer Science, with research focus on Extended Reality (XR), Internet of Things (IoT), and Machine Learning for enhancing disaster management and awareness.

I have a background in computer science and started my career as a Database Project Manager, where I gained practical experience working with data systems. This sparked my interest in data analytics and machine learning, using data to understand complex problems and find solutions.

Currently, I’m pursuing a PhD researching how technologies like Extended Reality (XR), IoT, and AI can improve disaster management by helping people make better, faster decisions in emergencies.

As a Lecturer at AAB College, I teach courses focused on data analytics and programming, aiming to equip students with practical skills that are relevant in today’s tech-driven world. I believe in making learning engaging and applicable, so students can connect what they study with real-life challenges.

I’m passionate about exploring how immersive technologies and intelligent systems can work together to create smarter, more adaptive solutions. Whether through teaching or research, my goal is to help shape technology that makes a positive impact.
